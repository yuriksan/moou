# PPTX Executive Roadmap Deck Redesign

## What We're Building

Redesign the PowerPoint roadmap export from a data-dump deck into a polished executive presentation with a two-act narrative structure: "Where we are" (delivery execution) and "Where we're going" (strategic direction). The deck should look like something a VP of Engineering or Product would present in a quarterly review, not a report generated by a tool.

## Why This Approach

The current deck (PR #5) established the right content — KPI cards, charts, risk cards, appendix. But the visual quality is low: flat backgrounds, basic shapes, Arial everywhere, monotonous layout. Enterprise roadmap decks use gradient headers, accent stripes, takeaway titles, swimlane timelines, and traffic-light status indicators. The narrative structure also needs work — the current deck doesn't distinguish between in-flight delivery and future strategy, which are the two questions executives actually ask.

## Key Decisions

- **Two-act narrative**: Act 1 = delivery execution (are we on track?), Act 2 = strategic direction (is the plan right?). Appendix for detail.
- **Takeaway titles**: Every slide title states the conclusion, not the topic. Generated from data.
- **Swimlane timeline**: Horizontal tracks per milestone with outcomes as blocks, replacing point markers.
- **8 main slides + appendix**: Down from ~16. Fewer, denser, more intentional.
- **Visual design system**: Dark gradient title, accent stripe on every slide, slide numbers, consistent typography (sans-serif headers, smaller body), branded color palette.
- **Phased delivery**:
  - **Phase A (now)**: Visual redesign + narrative restructure with templated text
  - **Phase B (after valueedge-sync-preview)**: Delivery progress data (child completion from VE) feeds into health dashboard, timeline progress bars, "Delivery Confidence" slide
  - **Phase C (later)**: Local LLM (Ollama in Docker) generates executive narrative from structured data — slide titles, status summaries, motivation-to-strategy narrative. Graceful fallback to templates.

## Slide Structure

### Slide 1: Title
- Dark gradient background (not flat)
- "Product Roadmap — Q2 2026"
- Organization/team name subtitle
- Footer: "Confidential · Generated YYYY-MM-DD · moou"

### Slide 2: "Where We Stand"
- One-slide health dashboard
- Top band: 3 KPI metrics (revenue at risk, on-track %, next compliance deadline) with traffic-light background colors
- Middle: single-sentence status summary
- Bottom: small doughnut chart showing portfolio mix

### Slide 3: "Current Delivery" (Act 1)
- Swimlane timeline — one horizontal track per active/upcoming milestone
- Outcomes as colored blocks within lanes (green/amber/red by status)
- Phase B: progress bars per outcome from VE child progress

### Slide 4: "Revenue at Stake" (Act 1)
- Horizontal bar chart — top 5 customers
- Takeaway title with total dollar figure
- Compact, one slide

### Slide 5: "What Needs Attention" (Act 1)
- 2-3 conflict cards with red left accent border
- Each: conflict description + recommended action
- The slide that makes the meeting productive

### Slide 6: "What's Ahead" (Act 2)
- Future milestones (not yet active)
- Upcoming pipeline — what's being planned and targeted dates
- Lighter visual treatment than Act 1 (planned, not committed)

### Slide 7: "Why This Roadmap" (Act 2)
- Portfolio balance doughnut
- Top motivations driving future work (not yet in active delivery)
- Phase C: AI-generated narrative connecting motivations to strategy

### Slide 8: "Risks on the Horizon" (Act 2)
- Forward-looking: compliance deadlines approaching, competitive gaps, tech debt accumulating
- Three-column card layout with big numbers

### Appendix
- All detailed tables (unchanged from current implementation)
